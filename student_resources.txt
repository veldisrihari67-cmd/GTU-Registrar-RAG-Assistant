SECTION 1: ACADEMIC SUPPORT

CS TUTORING CENTER

The GTU CS Tutoring Center, located in Engineering Hall Room B10, provides free, walk-in tutoring for all undergraduate CS courses. The center is staffed by advanced undergraduate tutors (selected through a competitive application process) and supervised by graduate teaching assistants. Tutoring is available for CS101 through CS301, covering programming debugging, homework problem sets, and exam preparation.

Operating Hours (Academic Year): Monday–Thursday: 9:00 AM – 8:00 PM Friday: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM Sunday: 1:00 PM – 6:00 PM

Tutors are assigned by course expertise. Students are encouraged to bring their laptop, course materials, and specific questions. General "help me understand everything" sessions are less productive than focused, specific questions. The center also maintains a Discord server (invite link available at the front desk) where students can post questions and receive asynchronous help from tutors and peers between sessions.

MATHEMATICS RESOURCE CENTER (MRC)

The MRC, located in the Mathematics Building Room 100, provides tutoring for all mathematics and statistics courses, including MATH110, MATH111, MATH220, MATH230, and STAT200. Walk-in tutoring is available; appointments with specific tutors can be scheduled through the GTU Student Services portal. The MRC also offers a Calculus Bootcamp at the beginning of each semester for students who need a rapid review of precalculus and early calculus concepts.

WRITING CENTER

The GTU Writing Center provides free, appointment-based writing support to all students. For CS and DS students, the Writing Center is particularly valuable for WRIT310 (Technical Writing), CS401 (Capstone Report), and any course requiring substantial written work. Writing Center consultants are trained to help with all stages of the writing process: planning and outlining, drafting, revising, and proofreading. Appointments can be made through the GTU Student Services portal up to one week in advance.

SECTION 2: ACADEMIC ADVISING
Every CS and DS student is assigned a faculty academic advisor in their department and a professional advisor in the GTU Office of Undergraduate Advising. Students are encouraged to meet with both advisors at least once per semester.

CS DEPARTMENT ADVISING OFFICE

Location: Engineering Hall, Room 205 Email: cs-advising@gtu.edu Drop-in Hours: Monday, Wednesday, Friday, 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM Appointment Scheduling: GTU Student Services portal (recommended for complex advising questions, prerequisite petitions, and graduation planning)

The CS advising office can assist with: course selection and sequencing, prerequisite waiver petitions, transfer credit inquiries, graduation requirement verification, study abroad planning, and referrals to other university resources.

MANDATORY ADVISING HOLDS

Advising holds are placed on all undergraduate CS and DS students' registration accounts before each enrollment period. A hold prevents registration until the student has met with their advisor and the advisor has released the hold through the GTU system. Students should schedule advising appointments at least three weeks before registration opens to ensure timely hold release. Advising holds are a feature, not a punishment — they ensure that every student makes course selections with the guidance of someone who knows their academic history and goals.

SECTION 3: STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS

GTU ASSOCIATION FOR COMPUTING MACHINERY (ACM) CHAPTER

The GTU ACM Chapter is the primary CS student organization at GTU, hosting weekly technical talks (by faculty, industry speakers, and student researchers), hackathons, coding competitions (including the annual GTU Coding Cup), and social events. The chapter organizes teams for the ACM International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) and coordinates the annual GTU career fair in the fall semester. All CS and DS students are encouraged to join (annual membership fee: $10, waived for students with demonstrated financial need).

GTU WOMEN IN COMPUTER SCIENCE (WiCS)

GTU WiCS is a student organization dedicated to supporting and building community among women and non-binary students in computing. The organization hosts mentorship programs pairing underclasswomen with senior student mentors, industry networking events, and workshops on topics including resume building, negotiating salary, and navigating the technology industry as a woman. WiCS also partners with local K-12 schools to run CS outreach programs for girls.

GTU CYBERSECURITY CLUB (GTU-Sec)

GTU-Sec is the home of GTU's competitive CTF team and cybersecurity enthusiasts more broadly. The club hosts weekly technical sessions (hands-on challenges in web exploitation, binary exploitation, cryptography, and forensics), organizes participation in external CTF competitions, and provides the mentorship pipeline for CS330 lab sections. The club maintains the GTU CyberArena, a dedicated virtual lab environment for safe security practice.

GTU DATA SCIENCE SOCIETY (DSS)

The DSS hosts workshops on data science tools and techniques (Pandas, Scikit-learn, SQL, Tableau, Power BI), organizes teams for Kaggle competitions, hosts a monthly data visualization showcase, and runs a peer mentorship program for DS major students. The society maintains relationships with industry data science teams and facilitates internship and full-time recruiting connections.

GTU ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE CLUB (GTUAI)

GTUAI hosts a weekly reading group on AI research papers, organizes paper-to-code replication projects, runs workshops on PyTorch and the Hugging Face ecosystem, and hosts the annual GTU AI Showcase — an event in which student ML projects are presented to a panel of industry judges. The club is particularly active in organizing study groups for the CS305 and CS310 courses.

SECTION 4: CAREER SERVICES

GTU CS CAREER CENTER

The GTU CS Career Center, located in Engineering Hall Room 110, provides dedicated career services for CS and DS students. Services include resume and cover letter review (with CS-specific feedback), mock technical interviews (both coding interviews and system design interviews), company information sessions, and the coordination of on-campus recruiting.

The Career Center strongly encourages students to begin career preparation in their second year, even if they are not planning to apply for internships immediately. A polished resume, a LinkedIn profile, and a GitHub portfolio with well-documented projects are foundational career assets that take time to develop.

INTERNSHIP AND CO-OP PROGRAM

GTU has established recruiting relationships with over 200 companies in the technology industry, from large technology companies (Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Meta) to mid-size software companies, fintech firms, healthcare technology companies, and startups. The annual GTU Tech Career Fair, held in September of each academic year, brings 80–100 employers to campus for on-site recruiting.

CS and DS students are strongly encouraged to complete at least one internship before graduation. The GTU CS Internship Data Report (published annually by the Career Center) documents internship offers accepted by GTU students, including company names, roles, and compensation. The median software engineering internship offer accepted by a GTU CS junior or senior in the most recent academic year was $8,500/month, with full-time conversion offers extending to new graduates in approximately 60% of cases.

SECTION 5: MENTAL HEALTH AND WELLNESS

The CS and DS programs at GTU are academically demanding, and the department takes the mental health and wellness of its students seriously. The following resources are available:

GTU COUNSELING CENTER: Free, confidential counseling services for all enrolled students. Same-day crisis appointments available. Location: Student Services Building, Room 200. Phone: (555) 867-5309. Crisis Line (24/7): (555) 867-5400.

GTU CS PEER SUPPORT NETWORK: A student-run program that trains advanced CS students as peer supporters — empathetic listeners who can provide a first line of support, share resources, and help connect struggling students to professional services. Peer supporters are identifiable by their blue GTU CS lanyards and are present at all major CS department events.

ACADEMIC ACCOMMODATIONS: Students with documented disabilities are entitled to reasonable academic accommodations. The GTU Office of Disability Services (ODS) coordinates accommodations including extended test time, distraction-reduced testing environments, note-taking assistance, and others. Students seeking accommodations should contact ODS as early as possible in the semester, as accommodation letters must be submitted to instructors within the first two weeks of classes for the accommodation to apply to initial assessments.
