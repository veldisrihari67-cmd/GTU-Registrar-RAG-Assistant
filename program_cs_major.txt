SECTION 1: PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (B.S. CS) at Global Tech University is a rigorous, four-year undergraduate program designed to equip students with deep theoretical foundations and extensive practical skills in computing. Graduates of this program are prepared for careers in software engineering, systems architecture, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, data science, and academic or industrial research.
The program combines a demanding core curriculum in computer science theory with hands-on laboratory experiences, interdisciplinary electives, and a culminating Senior Capstone Project. Students are encouraged to develop both technical excellence and ethical awareness, recognizing that the software and systems they build will profoundly shape human society.
Program accreditation is maintained through ABET (Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology), and the curriculum is reviewed and updated annually by the GTU Curriculum Committee in collaboration with the CS Industry Advisory Board.

SECTION 2: PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES

Upon successful completion of the B.S. in Computer Science, graduates will be able to:

1.Analyze, design, and implement algorithms and data structures appropriate to a given problem domain.
2.Apply principles of software engineering to develop reliable, scalable, and maintainable software systems.
3.Demonstrate understanding of computer organization, operating systems, and network architectures.
4.Design and evaluate database systems, including relational and non-relational models.
5.Apply mathematical and statistical reasoning to computational problems.
6.Develop solutions using multiple programming paradigms, including object-oriented, functional, and concurrent programming.
7.Identify and reason about ethical, legal, and societal implications of computing technologies.
8.Communicate complex technical ideas effectively in both written and oral forms.
9.Collaborate effectively in interdisciplinary team environments.
10.Demonstrate readiness for graduate study or professional practice in computing.

Students must complete at least four upper-division CS electives (300–400 level), with at least two from a single approved concentration track. Concentration tracks include:
Track A — Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning:
CS310: Deep Learning, CS315: Natural Language Processing, CS320: Computer Vision, CS325: Reinforcement Learning
Track B — Systems and Security:
CS330: Cybersecurity Fundamentals, CS335: Network Security, CS340: Cloud Computing, CS345: Embedded Systems
Track C — Data and Software Engineering:
CS350: Big Data Systems, CS355: Software Architecture, CS360: Human-Computer Interaction, CS365: Mobile Application Development

SECTION 9: ADVISING AND PROGRESSION MILESTONES

Students must meet with their assigned CS Academic Advisor at least once per semester. Mandatory advising holds are placed on registration accounts before each enrollment period and are released only upon advisor confirmation of satisfactory academic progress.
Milestone checkpoints:

End of Year 1: Confirm C+ or higher in CS101 to proceed to CS102
End of Year 2: Declare concentration track; confirm completion of all lower-division core
End of Year 3: Capstone readiness review with advisor; confirm GPA eligibility (2.50+)
Graduation Application: Filed by October 1 for May graduation; February 1 for August/December
