SECTION 2: PREREQUISITE DEPENDENCY CHAINS — VISUAL REFERENCE

The following chains represent the major prerequisite dependency paths in the CS major. Understanding these chains is essential for academic planning, particularly for students who enter the program with transfer credit, take a leave of absence, or fail a course at a critical junction.
CHAIN 1 — THE CORE CS SEQUENCE (Critical Path to Capstone)
MATH110 ──────────────────────────────┐
│
CS101 → [C+] → CS102 → [C] → CS201 → [C+C] → CS301 → [C] ─┐
↕              │
STAT200 [C]             CS305 → [C]
MATH230 [C]               │
│
CS305 [C] + CS301 [C] + 90cr + 2.50 GPA
↓
CS401 (CAPSTONE)
Legend: [C] = minimum grade C required; [C+] = minimum grade C+ required; [C+C] = CS102 requires C+, CS201 requires C from CS102
CHAIN 2 — THE SYSTEMS PATH (CS150 → CS210 → CS230 → CS330)
CS101 → [C] → CS150 → [C] ─┐
├──→ CS210 → [C] → CS230 → [C] → CS330
CS201 → [C] ────────────────┘
Note: Both CS150 and CS201 must be completed before CS210. The systems chain requires the most careful sequencing of any track, as each course is directly prerequisite to the next.
CHAIN 3 — THE MATHEMATICS CHAIN (Supporting Multiple CS Courses)
MATH110 → MATH111 (for DS)
MATH110 → MATH220/CS180 (required for CS290)
MATH110 → MATH230 (required for CS305)
MATH110 → STAT200 (required for CS301 Path B, CS305)
STAT200 → STAT310 → STAT320 (DS major statistics track)
CHAIN 4 — THE AI/ML TRACK (Upper Division Electives)
CS301 + STAT200 + MATH230 → CS305 → CS310 (Deep Learning)
→ CS315 (NLP)
→ CS320 (Computer Vision)
→ CS325 (Reinforcement Learning)
CHAIN 5 — THE SOFTWARE ENGINEERING TRACK
CS102 → CS160 → CS280 → CS355 (Software Architecture)
→ CS340 (Cloud Computing, with CS210)
→ CS360 (HCI)
→ CS365 (Mobile, with CS170 + CS190)



SECTION 3: PREREQUISITE LOGIC WORKED EXAMPLES

The following worked examples are provided to help students and advisors reason through the more complex prerequisite logic in the catalog. These examples cover all major AND/OR combinations and edge cases.
EXAMPLE SET 1 — CS102 PREREQUISITE (CS101 with C+)
Example 1A: Student earned B in CS101. → B (3.0) ≥ C+ (2.3). CS102 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 1B: Student earned C in CS101. → C (2.0) < C+ (2.3). CS102 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED. Student must retake CS101 to earn C+ or higher.
Example 1C: Student earned C+ in CS101 transfer equivalent. → Transfer equivalent accepted at C+ or higher. CS102 prerequisite SATISFIED, provided transfer credit has been officially evaluated and posted by the Office of Transfer Affairs before registration opens.
Example 1D: Student is currently enrolled in CS101 (in progress). → In-progress courses do not satisfy completed prerequisites. Student may not enroll in CS102 until CS101 final grade is posted. Exception: a student may register for CS102 during the registration period before CS101 grades are posted, with the understanding that the CRS will automatically drop them from CS102 if the final CS101 grade is below C+.
EXAMPLE SET 2 — CS201 PREREQUISITE (CS102 AND MATH110, both with C)
Example 2A: Student earned B+ in CS102 and B in MATH110. → Both conditions met. CS201 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 2B: Student earned A in CS102 but has not taken MATH110. → AND condition: MATH110 condition not met. CS201 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED.
Example 2C: Student earned C in CS102 and C in MATH110. → Both conditions met (C = 2.0 ≥ 2.0). CS201 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 2D: Student earned D+ in MATH110 and A in CS102. → MATH110 grade (D+, 1.3) is below required minimum (C, 2.0). CS201 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED. Student must retake MATH110.
Example 2E: Student earned C- in CS102 and B in MATH110. → CS102 grade (C-, 1.7) is below required minimum (C, 2.0). CS201 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED. Student must retake CS102.
EXAMPLE SET 3 — CS301 PREREQUISITE (CS201 OR [CS102 AND STAT200])
Example 3A: Student has CS201 with B. → PATH A satisfied. CS301 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 3B: Student has CS102 with A- and STAT200 with B+. Has NOT taken CS201. → PATH B satisfied (both CS102 ≥ C and STAT200 ≥ C). CS301 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 3C: Student has CS201 with D. Has CS102 with A and STAT200 with A. → PATH A NOT satisfied (CS201 grade below C). PATH B satisfied (CS102 ≥ C and STAT200 ≥ C). CS301 prerequisite SATISFIED via PATH B.
Example 3D: Student has CS102 with B but has NOT taken STAT200. Has NOT taken CS201. → PATH A NOT satisfied (CS201 not taken). PATH B NOT satisfied (STAT200 not taken). CS301 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED.
Example 3E: Student has CS201 with C and STAT200 with A. → PATH A satisfied (CS201 ≥ C). PATH B also technically satisfied, but only one path needs to be satisfied. CS301 prerequisite SATISFIED. Note: This student would benefit from STAT200 for CS305 preparation.
Example 3F: Student has CS201 with C-. → C- (1.7) < C (2.0). PATH A NOT satisfied. If student also lacks CS102+STAT200 combination, CS301 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED.
EXAMPLE SET 4 — CS401 PREREQUISITE (CS301 AND CS305 AND 90+ credits AND GPA ≥ 2.50)
Example 4A: Student has CS301 (B), CS305 (C), 94 credits, GPA 2.67. → All four conditions met. CS401 prerequisite SATISFIED.
Example 4B: Student has CS301 (B), CS305 (C), 94 credits, GPA 2.43. → GPA condition (2.43 < 2.50) NOT met. CS401 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED. Student must raise GPA before enrolling.
Example 4C: Student has CS301 (B), CS305 (B), 88 credits, GPA 3.1. → Credit hour condition (88 < 90) NOT met at registration. If by the start of the semester the student will have completed 90 credits (e.g., through current enrollment), the student should consult the Registrar. Note that 90 credits must be completed and posted, not merely in progress.
Example 4D: Student has CS301 (C) but CS305 grade is not yet posted (in progress). → CS305 condition not yet met (in-progress courses do not count). CS401 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED until CS305 grade is posted at C or higher.
Example 4E: Student has CS301 (D) and CS305 (B+), 95 credits, GPA 2.6. → CS301 grade (D, 1.0) < C (2.0). CS301 condition NOT met. CS401 prerequisite NOT SATISFIED. Student must retake CS301.

SECTION 4: COMMON PREREQUISITE QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS

Q: I took CS101 at another university and earned a B. Can I enroll in CS102?
A: Potentially yes, but only after the transfer credit has been officially evaluated and posted by the Office of Transfer Affairs and the equivalent course has been accepted as the CS101 equivalent with a grade of C+ or higher. You cannot use in-process transfer credit to satisfy prerequisites. If the transfer course was accepted but at a grade below C+ equivalent, you must take CS101 at GTU.

Q: I earned a C in MATH110 two years ago. I want to take CS201. Can I?
A: Yes. There is no time limit on the validity of completed prerequisites at GTU, provided the course content has not changed substantially. However, if you have been away from calculus for two years, you are strongly encouraged to review MATH110 material before enrolling in CS201, as the asymptotic analysis content assumes calculus fluency.

Q: I have CS102 with a B and STAT200 with a B. Can I register for CS301 even though I haven't taken CS201?
A: Yes. PATH B of the CS301 prerequisite is: CS102 (grade C or higher) AND STAT200 (grade C or higher). Your B grades in both courses satisfy PATH B. You do not need CS201 to enroll in CS301, though completing CS201 before or concurrently with CS301 is strongly recommended and will substantially deepen your understanding of the course material.

Q: I earned a C in CS301 but a D in CS305 last semester. I want to retake CS305. Can I?
A: Yes. You may retake CS305 as you have completed CS301 with a C (satisfying the CS301 prerequisite) and STAT200 and MATH230 (assumed from your first CS305 enrollment). Only the higher of your two CS305 grades will be included in your GPA calculation. Both attempts will appear on your transcript.

Q: Can I take CS301 and STAT200 concurrently to satisfy PATH B of CS301's prerequisite?
A: No. Concurrent enrollment in STAT200 does not satisfy the CS301 prerequisite for PATH B. The prerequisite requires STAT200 to be completed (grade posted) before CS301 enrollment. You must complete STAT200 in one semester and enroll in CS301 in a subsequent semester.

Q: I need CS210 for my degree plan but I failed CS150 and need to retake it. How does this affect my timeline?
A: Significantly. CS210 requires both CS150 AND CS201. Retaking CS150 delays your CS210 enrollment by at least one semester, which in turn delays CS230 (which requires CS210), CS330 (which requires CS230), and any other courses in the systems chain. Meet with your advisor immediately to develop a modified four-year plan that accommodates the retake.

Q: I have 89 credits and want to register for CS401 next semester, during which I will complete 15 more credits, giving me 104 total. Does the 90-credit requirement look at credits at the time of registration or at the time the semester begins?
A: The 90-credit requirement is checked at the time of enrollment (when you register), and the CRS checks the number of completed and posted credits at that moment. 89 posted credits does not satisfy the 90-credit threshold. However, if you are registering during a period when your current semester's credits have not yet been posted (e.g., you are registering for next fall while completing spring credits), you may petition the Registrar's Office for a manual review, providing your current enrollment and expected completion. In most cases, if you will clearly have 90+ credits before the start of the CS401 semester, the Registrar will process a manual enrollment with the stipulation that automatic withdrawal applies if the credit threshold is not met.

SECTION 6: CATALOG AUTHORITY AND AMENDMENT PROCEDURES

This catalog represents the official academic regulations, course offerings, and degree requirements of Global Tech University for the 2025–2026 academic year. Students who begin a degree program under this catalog are generally entitled to complete their degree under the requirements stated herein, provided they maintain continuous enrollment (no absence of more than two consecutive semesters excluding summer). Students who interrupt their enrollment for more than two consecutive semesters may be required to complete degree requirements under the catalog in effect at the time of their return.

Amendments to catalog requirements may be made by the GTU Academic Senate with majority approval and the concurrence of the Provost. Amendments that increase requirements (requiring more courses, higher grades, or additional criteria for existing students) must include a grandfather provision protecting students already enrolled in the affected program. Amendments that decrease requirements (relaxing standards) apply immediately to all enrolled students unless the Academic Senate specifies otherwise.

Errors or discrepancies discovered in this catalog should be reported to the Office of the Academic Registrar at registrar@gtu.edu. In cases of discrepancy between this catalog and the GTU Course Registration System, the Academic Registrar will issue a written determination within ten business days. During the resolution period, the more student-favorable interpretation will be applied as a default.

The GTU Academic Catalog is published annually each June for the academic year beginning the following August. The official electronic version, available at catalog.gtu.edu, is the authoritative document. Printed copies are provided as a convenience and may not reflect late amendments made after printing. Students are encouraged to check catalog.gtu.edu for the most current version.
