SECTION 1: PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The Bachelor of Science in Data Science (B.S. DS) at Global Tech University is an interdisciplinary program at the intersection of computer science, mathematics, statistics, and domain-specific knowledge. Designed for students who wish to extract meaning from large, complex datasets and build predictive models that drive real-world decisions, the Data Science major integrates quantitative rigor with computational power and communicative clarity.
Data Science graduates from GTU are equipped to work in industries ranging from healthcare and finance to environmental science and public policy. The program emphasizes not only technical proficiency in statistical modeling, machine learning, and data engineering, but also the development of critical thinking skills necessary to interpret results responsibly, communicate findings to diverse audiences, and navigate the ethical complexities of data-driven decision making.
The program is jointly administered by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering and the Department of Mathematics and Statistics, ensuring that students receive balanced training in both computational and mathematical domains. A capstone research project in the final year allows students to apply their integrated skill set to a substantial, original data science challenge.

SECTION 2: PROGRAM LEARNING OUTCOMES

Upon successful completion of the B.S. in Data Science, graduates will be able to:

Collect, clean, integrate, and manage large-scale datasets from diverse sources.
Apply statistical inference, hypothesis testing, and probabilistic modeling to real-world data problems.
Design, implement, and evaluate machine learning and deep learning models.
Use programming languages (Python, R, SQL) and data tools proficiently for analysis and visualization.
Communicate analytical findings through clear visualizations, reports, and presentations to technical and non-technical audiences.
Reason about the ethical, legal, and societal implications of data collection and algorithmic decision-making.
Apply domain knowledge from at least one application area (e.g., healthcare, business, environment).
Conduct independent data science research, including literature review, methodology design, and results interpretation.
Collaborate in interdisciplinary teams on complex data projects.
Demonstrate readiness for graduate study or professional practice in data science or related fields.

SECTION 9: DISTINCTION FROM CS MAJOR

While the B.S. CS and B.S. DS programs share foundational courses in programming and algorithms, they diverge significantly at the upper-division level. CS majors receive deeper training in systems programming, theory of computation, and software engineering. DS majors receive deeper training in statistical inference, data engineering, machine learning, and domain-specific applications. A student interested in both fields should consult with advisors from both departments about the possibility of pursuing a dual degree or a CS major with a Data Science minor.
