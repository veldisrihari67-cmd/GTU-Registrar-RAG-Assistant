GLOBAL TECH UNIVERSITY (GTU)
OFFICIAL ACADEMIC CATALOG — ACADEMIC YEAR 2025–2026
OFFICE OF THE ACADEMIC REGISTRAR

ACADEMIC POLICIES AND REGULATIONS

SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION AND INSTITUTIONAL OVERVIEW
Global Tech University (GTU) is a premier institution dedicated to advancing technological education, interdisciplinary research, and ethical innovation. Founded on the principles of academic rigor, inclusive excellence, and global citizenship, GTU prepares students to lead in an increasingly complex and technology-driven world. The policies outlined in this document govern all undergraduate programs and serve as the binding framework for academic conduct, progression, and completion.
All students, faculty, and staff are expected to familiarize themselves with these regulations. Ignorance of a stated policy does not constitute grounds for exemption. Questions regarding interpretation or application of any policy should be directed to the Office of the Academic Registrar (OAR) or the relevant academic department.

SECTION 2: GRADING POLICY

2.1 GRADING SCALE
GTU employs a four-point grading scale. The following letter grades, grade points, and percentage equivalents are used across all undergraduate programs:
A+  = 4.0  (97–100%)
A   = 4.0  (93–96%)
A-  = 3.7  (90–92%)
B+  = 3.3  (87–89%)
B   = 3.0  (83–86%)
B-  = 2.7  (80–82%)
C+  = 2.3  (77–79%)
C   = 2.0  (73–76%)
C-  = 1.7  (70–72%)
D+  = 1.3  (67–69%)
D   = 1.0  (60–66%)
F   = 0.0  (Below 60%)
Additional non-punitive marks include:
I   = Incomplete (see Section 2.4)
W   = Withdrawal (see Section 2.5)
AU  = Audit (no credit awarded)
P/NP = Pass/No Pass (available for designated electives only)

2.2 GRADE POINT AVERAGE (GPA) CALCULATION
A student's cumulative GPA is calculated by dividing the total number of quality points earned by the total number of credit hours attempted. Transfer credits accepted for course equivalency do not factor into the GTU cumulative GPA unless the student formally petitions for inclusion through the Office of Transfer Affairs.
Minimum GPA requirements by classification:

Freshman (0–29 credits): 1.80 minimum to remain in good standing
Sophomore (30–59 credits): 2.00 minimum
Junior (60–89 credits): 2.00 minimum
Senior (90+ credits): 2.00 minimum; 2.50 required for Capstone enrollment

Students falling below the minimum GPA thresholds will be placed on Academic Probation for one semester. Failure to recover the required GPA within that probationary period may result in Academic Suspension for one full academic year.

2.3 GRADE PREREQUISITES AND MINIMUM GRADE RULES
Certain courses at GTU require a student to have earned a minimum grade in a prerequisite course before enrollment is permitted. These minimums are enforced automatically by the GTU Course Registration System (CRS) and cannot be overridden without a formal Prerequisite Waiver petition approved by both the course instructor and the relevant Department Chair.
Key minimum grade rules:

CS102 (Data Structures): Requires CS101 with a minimum grade of C+ (2.3)
CS201 (Algorithms): Requires CS102 AND MATH110, both with minimum grade of C
CS301 (Artificial Intelligence): Requires CS201 with grade C or higher, OR requires both CS102 AND STAT200 each with grade C or higher
CS401 (Senior Capstone): Requires CS301 AND CS305 AND completion of 90 or more credit hours, with a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.50

The AND/OR prerequisite logic is strictly enforced. A student who satisfies only one branch of an OR requirement must still fully satisfy that branch. Partial satisfaction of an AND requirement does not permit enrollment.

2.4 INCOMPLETE GRADES
An Incomplete (I) grade may be assigned when a student has completed the majority of coursework (at least 75%) but is unable to finish the remaining requirements due to a documented, verifiable emergency occurring near the end of the semester. The student must submit an Incomplete Grade Contract, co-signed by the instructor and Department Chair, no later than the final day of classes.
The default deadline for resolving an Incomplete is the end of the subsequent full semester (excluding summer sessions). If the Incomplete is not resolved within this period, the grade automatically converts to an F. Extensions may be granted only under extraordinary circumstances with written approval from the Academic Dean.

2.5 COURSE WITHDRAWAL
Students may withdraw from a course without academic penalty during the first two weeks of classes. Withdrawals between Week 3 and Week 10 result in a W notation on the transcript, which carries no GPA impact but counts toward the attempted credit total. After Week 10, a student may not withdraw from a course except in cases of documented medical or family emergency, subject to approval by the Academic Appeals Committee.
Students who accumulate more than three W grades in a single academic year, or more than six W grades over their undergraduate career, will be required to meet with an Academic Advisor and may be placed on an Academic Improvement Plan.

SECTION 3: CREDIT HOUR LIMITS AND ENROLLMENT POLICIES

3.1 STANDARD CREDIT LOAD
The standard full-time credit load for GTU undergraduates is 12 to 18 credit hours per semester. Students wishing to enroll in more than 18 credit hours in a single semester must obtain an Overload Approval from their Academic Advisor and must demonstrate a cumulative GPA of 3.3 or higher. The absolute maximum credit load per semester, under any circumstances, is 21 credit hours.
Part-time status is defined as enrollment in fewer than 12 credit hours per semester. Part-time students must be aware of the implications for financial aid eligibility, visa status (for international students), and academic progress timelines.
3.2 SUMMER AND WINTER SESSION LIMITS
During summer sessions, the maximum credit load is 9 credit hours per session. During winter intersession, the maximum is 4 credit hours. These limits cannot be overridden regardless of academic standing.
3.3 REPEAT COURSE POLICY
Students may repeat a course in which they earned a grade of D+ or below, or in which they earned a W. Upon repetition, only the higher of the two grades will be included in the GPA calculation; however, both attempts will remain visible on the official transcript. A course may not be repeated more than twice. Students seeking a third attempt must petition the Academic Standards Committee.

SECTION 4: TRANSFER CREDIT POLICIES

4.1 GENERAL TRANSFER PRINCIPLES
GTU accepts transfer credits from regionally accredited colleges and universities. Credits earned at nationally accredited institutions are evaluated on a case-by-case basis by the Office of Transfer Affairs in consultation with the appropriate academic department. International transfer credits require a credential evaluation from a NACES-approved agency.
A maximum of 64 semester credit hours may be transferred from a two-year (community college) institution. No limit applies to transfers from four-year institutions, provided total transfer credits do not exceed 90 of the minimum 120 credits required for graduation.
4.2 COURSE EQUIVALENCY DETERMINATION
Transfer courses are evaluated for equivalency based on course content, level, and learning outcomes. Students must submit course descriptions, syllabi, and, when available, sample assessments for departmental review. The following timeframes apply:

Standard equivalency determination: 15 business days
Expedited determination (with complete documentation): 7 business days

Courses without a direct GTU equivalent may be accepted as elective credit (labeled ELC-XXX on the GTU transcript) but may not satisfy specific major or core requirements unless a Substitution Petition is approved.
4.3 TRANSFER PREREQUISITE SATISFACTION
Transfer courses accepted as equivalents to GTU prerequisite courses will satisfy the prerequisite requirement, provided the student earned the minimum required grade in the transfer course. The same minimum grade rules that apply to GTU courses apply to transferred equivalents. The burden of proof lies with the student to demonstrate grade equivalency.

SECTION 5: CO-REQUISITE AND CONCURRENT ENROLLMENT RULES

5.1 CO-REQUISITE DEFINED
A co-requisite is a course that must be taken concurrently in the same semester as the course it supports. Unlike prerequisites, which must be completed before enrollment, co-requisites are designed to provide parallel support and context. Co-requisite pairings at GTU include:

CS102 Lab (CS102L): Must be taken concurrently with CS102 in the same semester. Enrollment in CS102 without CS102L is not permitted except when transferring CS102L equivalent credit.
MATH110 and MATH110R (Recitation): Students who score below the placement threshold on the GTU Mathematics Placement Assessment must enroll in MATH110R concurrently with MATH110. MATH110R carries 1 credit hour and is graded P/NP.
CS401 Capstone Seminar (CS401S): All students enrolled in CS401 must concurrently enroll in CS401S, the associated weekly seminar. CS401S is a 0-credit, mandatory co-requisite.

5.2 CO-REQUISITE WAIVER
A student who has previously completed a course designated as a co-requisite (at GTU or at a transfer institution) is not required to re-enroll in the co-requisite. Documentation of prior completion must be submitted to the Registrar's Office before the start of the semester.

SECTION 6: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY

GTU maintains an unwavering commitment to academic honesty. All forms of academic dishonesty — including plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, facilitating academic dishonesty, and unauthorized use of artificial intelligence tools for graded submissions — are subject to disciplinary action under the GTU Student Code of Conduct. Penalties range from a zero on the assignment to expulsion, depending on the severity and history of the violation. All suspected violations must be reported to the Office of Academic Integrity within 10 business days of discovery.

SECTION 7: GRADUATION REQUIREMENTS

To be eligible for graduation, students must:

1.Complete a minimum of 120 credit hours
2.Satisfy all major, minor (if applicable), and GTU Core Curriculum requirements
3.Maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 (2.5 for Honors designation)
4.Complete at least 30 of the final 45 credits in residence at GTU
5.Submit a completed Application for Graduation no later than the semester prior                       to the intended graduation term
6.Resolve all financial obligations to the university
7.Complete the GTU Senior Exit Survey and Assessment

Students completing degree requirements in fewer than four years are not automatically graduated; they must apply for graduation and meet all listed requirements. Degrees are conferred three times per year: May, August, and December.
