DEPARTMENTAL LEADERSHIP

DEPARTMENT CHAIR Dr. Amelia R. Thornton, Ph.D. Ph.D., Computer Science, Carnegie Mellon University, 2005 Research Areas: Distributed Systems, Cloud Computing, Fault-Tolerant Systems Office: Engineering Hall, Room 301 Email: a.thornton@gtu.edu Office Hours: Tuesday 2:00–4:00 PM, Thursday 2:00–4:00 PM

Dr. Thornton joined GTU in 2008 following three years as a Research Scientist at Google. Her research group, the GTU Distributed Systems Lab (DSL), investigates consistency and availability trade-offs in large-scale distributed databases, Byzantine fault tolerance in consensus protocols, and the performance modeling of cloud computing workloads. She has published more than 60 peer-reviewed papers and holds four U.S. patents. She was named an ACM Fellow in 2022.

ASSOCIATE CHAIR FOR UNDERGRADUATE AFFAIRS Dr. Marcus J. Osei, Ph.D. Ph.D., Computer Science, University of Michigan, 2010 Research Areas: CS Education, Broadening Participation in Computing, Pedagogical Technology Office: Engineering Hall, Room 215 Email: m.osei@gtu.edu Office Hours: Monday 1:00–3:00 PM, Friday 10:00 AM–12:00 PM

Dr. Osei oversees all undergraduate program administration, advising systems, and curriculum review. His research investigates evidence-based pedagogical practices in CS education, with particular focus on strategies for improving outcomes for first-generation college students and students from underrepresented groups in computing. He directs the GTU BRIDGE Program, a summer bridge experience for incoming CS students who are the first in their families to attend college.


FULL-TIME TEACHING FACULTY — CORE CS COURSES

Dr. Priya Subramaniam, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, Stanford University, 2009
Courses Taught: CS101, CS102, CS190
Research Areas: Programming Language Design, Type Systems, CS Pedagogy
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 118
Dr. Subramaniam is the lead instructor for the CS101 and CS102 sequence. She joined GTU in 2011 and has developed the current CS101 curriculum, including the transition from Java to Python as the introductory language, which was adopted in 2016 based on her pedagogical research. She is a recipient of the GTU Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching Award (2019, 2023) and a nationally recognized leader in computing education research.

Dr. Kwame Asante-Mensah, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, MIT, 2007
Courses Taught: CS201, CS290
Research Areas: Algorithms, Combinatorial Optimization, Computational Complexity
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 312
Dr. Asante-Mensah's research focuses on approximation algorithms for NP-hard optimization problems, with applications in operations research and network design. He has published extensively in the proceedings of STOC, FOCS, and SODA — the top theory venues — and serves on the editorial board of the Journal of the ACM. His CS201 course is consistently rated among the most demanding and most rewarding in the CS curriculum.

Dr. Yuki Nakamura, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, UC Berkeley, 2012
Courses Taught: CS301, CS305, CS310
Research Areas: Machine Learning, Reinforcement Learning, AI Safety
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 224
Dr. Nakamura directs the GTU AI and Machine Learning Lab (AIML Lab), one of the most active research groups in the department. Her research investigates safe and reliable reinforcement learning, with applications to robotic control and autonomous systems. She has received funding from NSF, DARPA, and multiple industry partners. She is a founding member of the ML Safety Scholars program, which funds undergraduate research assistants interested in AI safety.

Dr. Oluwaseun Adeleke, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, Georgia Tech, 2011
Courses Taught: CS210, CS230, CS330
Research Areas: Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Network Security
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 205
Dr. Adeleke's research focuses on operating system support for high-performance networking and on network security, particularly on the design of intrusion detection systems that are robust to adversarial evasion. He directs the GTU CyberSecurity Research Center and the undergraduate GTU CyberArena lab. He serves as faculty advisor for the GTU Capture the Flag (CTF) team, which has placed in the top ten at multiple national competitions.

Dr. Fatima Al-Rashidi, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, University of Toronto, 2013
Courses Taught: CS220, CS280
Research Areas: Database Systems, Data Engineering, Stream Processing
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 310
Dr. Al-Rashidi's research investigates the design of database systems for streaming data, with applications in financial systems, IoT, and real-time analytics. She has collaborated extensively with industry partners and served as a database systems researcher at Amazon Web Services before joining GTU. Her CS220 course is known for its rigorous treatment of transaction management and its integration of PostgreSQL lab exercises.

Dr. Samuel Okoye, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, University of Edinburgh, 2008
Courses Taught: CS150, CS250
Research Areas: Computer Architecture, Compilers, RISC-V Ecosystem
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 109
Dr. Okoye is a leading expert in RISC-V processor design and has contributed to the open-source RISC-V hardware and software ecosystem. His CS150 course uses RISC-V assembly throughout and includes a lab component in which students design a simple pipelined processor in a hardware description language (HDL). His CS250 course includes a substantial compiler project component that is legendary in the department for its rigor and its reward.

Dr. Ingrid Kowalczyk, Ph.D.
Ph.D., Computer Science, ETH Zurich, 2014
Courses Taught: CS160, CS315, CS320
Research Areas: Natural Language Processing, Human-Computer Interaction, AI Ethics
Office: Engineering Hall, Room 218
Dr. Kowalczyk's research focuses on natural language interfaces, including conversational AI systems, and on the evaluation of NLP systems for fairness and robustness. She has published in ACL, EMNLP, and NAACL, the top NLP venues, and has received the NSF CAREER Award. She is one of the faculty leads of the GTU Ethics in AI Initiative, which coordinates research and education on AI ethics across the university.
